Information Security Basics

  • Confidentiality - Ensuring data is accessible only to authorized users
  • Integrity - Maintaining data accuracy and trustworthiness
  • Availability - Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed
  • Authentication - Verifying the identity of users and systems
  • Authorization - Determining access rights to resources

Phishing Awareness

Phishing attacks are cyber threats where attackers impersonate legitimate entities (e.g., banks, companies, or government agencies) to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card details, or personal data. These attacks often come through emails, fake websites, phone calls, or text messages.

  • What is phising?

Preventative Measures

  • Verify Sources: Always check the sender's email address and avoid clicking on suspicious links.
  • Look for Red Flags: Spelling errors, urgent requests, or unfamiliar greetings can indicate phishing attempts.
  • Hover Over Links: Before clicking, hover over links to check if they lead to legitimate websites.
  • Keep Software Updated: Security updates help protect against vulnerabilities.
  • Never Share Sensitive Information: Legitimate organizations won’t ask for passwords or financial details via email or text.

Password Awareness & Protection

Passwords are the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. Strong, unique passwords help protect sensitive information from cyber threats.

Things to do:

  • Passwords are the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. Strong, unique passwords help protect sensitive information from cyber threats.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Adds an extra layer of security beyond just a password.
  • Use a Password Manager: Helps store and generate secure passwords instead of reusing them.
  • Change Passwords if Compromised: Update credentials immediately if you suspect a breach.

Things to avoid:

  • Reusing Passwords: A single compromised password can lead to multiple account breaches.
  • Using Personal Information: Avoid birthdays, names, or common words that hackers can easily guess.
  • Sharing Passwords: Keep credentials private and never share them via email or text.
  • Writing Passwords Down: Instead, use a secure password manager to store them safely.
